Why AXO?

Recruitment with Alpha Chi

When a young woman accepts a bid for membership in Alpha Chi Omega, she is welcomed and accepted as she is, and she will never be expected to fit a specific mold. Potential new members are selected for membership in Alpha Chi Omega based on our national standards of membership: character, leadership ability, academic interest, personal development and financial responsibility. These standards shape our members' experience in Alpha Chi Omega and help them reach their personal and professional goals.

Starting with bid night the Monday after recruitment, new members are welcomed into the sisterhood by older members, bond with their New Member Class, and learn about the history and values of Alpha Chi Omega, both nationally and locally at CWRU. The new member period is filled with excitement and great memories, and is only the beginning to lifelong membership in Alpha Chi Omega.


What we stand for

At AXO, we're all about being and finding real, strong women; its been our national slogan since 2008, and our reality since our very founding in 1885. Being an Alpha Chi means being part of an organization that allows you to be yourself, make the most of your life, and helping you to realize your full potential- whether during your time at Case or after graduation.

Alpha Chi means every sister receives the encouragement, inspiration, fun, and friendship of over 200,000 other sisters nationwide. It means every sister is a part of a learning and leadership program supplementing and complementing what she gains from Case's classrooms. As an Alpha Chi, you will have an opportunity to make a difference in your own life, the lives of your sisters, and the lives of those in the community around you.
