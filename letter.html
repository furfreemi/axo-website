From Our President


Greetings!

Greek Life here at Case Western Reserve University is very unique and there is a sense of camaraderie, where we want to see each other succeed. Those same ideals that our community holds, is true within the Zeta Upsilon chapter of Alpha Chi Omega here at Case. The sisterhood that has been established on campus for over 30 years has stayed strong through the values that we uphold, which are: personal development, character, financial responsibility, academic interest, and leadership ability. When combined, these values are what unite all 230,000 Alpha Chi's across the nation.

We strive to create a balanced experience between academics and the creating a sisterhood. Our chapter is comprised of a wide variety of majors which includes, engineers, nursing, biochemistry, and business. We are able use our diversity of majors to our advantage and assign academic mentors to younger members to foster relationships and ideal that academics are important, to just name an example. Our sisterhood event allows members to try something new, whether it be ice skating in the winter or going downtown to watch Wicked together in Play House Square, there is always something fun to try out. We also have mixers with other chapters or host social events like Sweaters and Service, where we invite the entire campus to participate in community service in December, to develop and strengthen relations with different organizations and chapters on campus.

We are very proud of our philanthropy, Domestic Violence Awareness, in which we dedicate an entire week in October to raise awareness and money for the Domestic Violence and Child Advocacy Center of Greater Cleveland (DVCAC). This week long event is created to educate our community about domestic violence and the impacts that it can have in our community. Not only are we passionate of our own philanthropy, members take an active part in being involve and participate in other philanthropies on campus.

Joining the chapter has been an unbelievable experience, one that has really enhanced my college experience. The mentors that I found and the lifelong friendships that I've built have been a huge part of my time here. Meeting women in Alpha Chi, that are also heavily involved in other organizations on campus has been inspiring and humbling. It has been a privilige serving as chapter president for the Zeta Upsilon chapter and I believe that I've made my experience in Alpha Chi Omega truly my own and that everyone is equipped also equipped with the power to do so. Please feel free to look around our website to learn more about our chapter.

Sincerely,

Stephanie Chen
Former President, ZY Chapter of Alpha Chi Omega
